Abstract

GraphOmni introduces a 242 k-query benchmark that probes large language models on six classical graph-theoretic problems under nine prompting strategies, seven text serializations, and three difficulty tiers. Evaluations across ten open- and closed-source LLMs reveal substantial performance gaps between local and global graph reasoning and show that input representation is as pivotal as model choice. To mitigate exhaustive combinatorial evaluation, we present RL-Opt, a reinforcement-learning search that discovers near-optimal (prompt, serialization, model) triples while reducing query cost by 75 %. We release the dataset, code, and leaderboard to foster progress on graph-savvy LLMs.
